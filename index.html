Evolve.exe

Couch Arcade Shooter, where players fight against each other to become the ultimate Host of the Server.

Unity C# 2024-2025
View Details

MULTIPLAYER, SPLIT SCREEN, ARCADE SHOOTER

As a testament to those good old days when people gathered around a couch to play a single game where players battle against each other. In short: each player gets to decide their own playstyle; they each get to choose what wepaon they weild, what engine they wear, and what gadget they use.

Weapons

Even though there is two types of weapons: ranged and melee, each wepaon has their own unique mechanics, and they all feel different to use. Some are serve as counters for others, but they all have their own strengths and weaknesses.

Engines

Engines will determine how the player moves around the map. Either, taking advantage of the verticality of the map or just simply getting around faster.

Gadgets

Gadgets are items that provide utility to the player, either in compbat or in movement. Providing addiional ways of getting around the map. They provide the player the space control that the engines dont provide themselves.

Exibiting the Project

I had the opportunity to exhibit Evolve.exe at Goldsmiths University's Computing Degree Show 2024, where I got to see people playing the game and having fun with it. It was a great experience to see how people interacted with the game and how they enjoyed it.

Games AI

Can a simple set of rules beat a human?

C# Unity Heuristics Games AI
View Details

Hueristic Based AI for Tetris

Although used for tetris, Heursitics are a way of leveriging human strategy in a way way computers can understand. This is by creating functions, that allow the AI to make decisions based on a "choice score". Meaning that for each choise the AI has to make, it will determine which is the best for it.

This was a submission for a Games AI Course given by Alan Zucconi. The goal of this project was simply to create an AI that would score as High as Possible, using what we learned in class. Therefore, we were given a tetris template on Unity, with a couple tools for previwing our score, anylizing our AIs gameplay, and comparing different strategies. However, most programming and logic was made using Tetris API, and lambda functions.

More Details

I took a heursitic aproach. But I am not a profesional Tetris player, so not only did I start playing a lot, but I also began extensive research on well known heuristic strategies out there. I tried each on their own to see how well they socred, and then I weighted them to put them together.

Machine Learning For Creative Practice

Python Meadia Pipe C# Maya HDRP
View Details

Torii - An Interactive Expireince

This was a project for a course I took at Goldsmiths University, called: Data and Machine Learning for Creative Practice. The goal of the project was to create a interactive installation using machine learning. We decided to use mediapipe to track the users hand and feed the data into a LSTM model that would generate new hand movements, which would then be visualized in Unity. It was a really fun project that allowed me to explore the creative possibilities of machine learning.

More Details

First thing and most important for this project was to make a python script calling the (handtracking) MediaPipe API. This script connects the camara to google's hand tracking neural network, and returns a set of positions for each land mark in the hand, in a 2D frame. I oass in the data into anothe rufnciton that inerprets it and cleans it up so I can send it straight into Unity.

I decided to use unity since I can leverage its tools and the asset store to combine a set of visuals and tools that uplift the expirience compelty. Leveraging the power of tools like animation controller, game object colliders, and rigid body physics I can create: a realistic enviroment, flame projectiles with glow, animal animation with a ghost shader, and a hand wireframe

BLIND

Listen Touch Explore

C# Unity Shaders
View Details

SHORT MEDITATIVE GAME

Blind is a short meditative game, meant to be played with headphones. There is no winning, or loosing, rather the goal of the game is simply to listen and explore. Around the cave, there are multiple items you can find, which have distinct sounds. You could try to find them all, or just find the ones you like the most. The game was made in a week and was made fore the sole purpose of creating a meditatice expireince.

More Details

Inspiration

In the world of Avatar the Last Airbender, Toph Beifong is and a blind Earthbender introduced in season 2 of the series. Earthbending, and any type of “Bending” is this world’s “Magic”. Characters are able to manipulate and the elements to their will at proximity. Born blind, Toph was overly mothered by her parents which pushed her away to them into discovering earthbending on her own. Being taught by blind animals, she eventually learned to “see” as long as she has ground.

The first thing I did was test out how it would be to be a fish, in a coral reef. Snooping and swimming around, blind, trying to find food and run away from pay. First thing I did was the shader, which is an intersection shader I used for another game, and it worked perfectly with this game. Next I did the swimming. I then realized that the effect was satisfying to look at, specially in organic geometry, and enclosed geometry like a cave.

I realized: The game does not need, enemies, external pressure or anything. Looking at this effect, listening to sounds, and moving around was enough. It felt like ZEN.

Daniel + Toph

Like DANIEL, there are many people who actively use Echolocation to navigate around the world. So even if TOPH used “magic” she could be real… why not make a game about her?

What if I make about a girl “lost” exploring a cave. Instead of being scared of the sounds, (an average child would be scared of), she is intrigued and attracted to them. But like TOPH’s parents, her parents are worried looking for her. Will she come out of the cave as soon as she hears them… or will she keep exploring until she gets bored?

In the future I would like to explore the idea of sound in video games. In this case it made for a satusfying expirience, but I would like to explore the idea of sound as a game mechanic more deeply. Maybe even as an accesibility feuture. SO i really enjoyed the video following video.
